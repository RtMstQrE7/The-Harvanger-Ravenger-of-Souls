The Harvenger: Ravenger of Souls - Game Review (Cleaned)
I. User Interface (UI) and Visibility Issues
The current UI severely hinders gameplay and clarity.

Floating, Resizable Windows (Crucial!): The Game Log and the Turn/Phase Indicator are difficult to see on PC. These elements should be placed in separate, opaque, resizable, and movable floating windows. They must float over the main screen at all times, even when the screen is scrolled.

Card Inspection: Clicking on a card should fully expand it, showing a clear image, the complete rules text, and the mana cost. The current large ID display is insufficient.

Main Game Board Size: The main game board should always be visible in its entirety. A magnifying option (e.g., right-click to zoom) should be available for specific areas or cards.

Draw Notification: When the system automatically draws a card for the player, there is no clear notification. The player should be explicitly informed, perhaps via a pop-up or a clear log entry.

Card Text Visibility: A card appeared during the Upkeep phase that targets a creature, but the player could not zoom in or see its text clearly, rendering it useless.

II. Gameplay Mechanics and Card Interaction
The core mechanisms for playing, tapping, and untapping are unclear and buggy, leading to game-breaking issues.

Mana Cost Clarity (Color System): The notation system for mana costs, such as "1W," is confusing. A number (e.g., '1') should represent Colorless Mana (mana of any type), and a letter (e.g., 'W') should represent Colored Mana (mana from a specific color land, e.g., a Planes land for White). The description of a card's cost must be clearer.

Tapping/Untapping Lands: There is no visual indicator that a land is tapped (e.g., turning the card sideways). This needs to be implemented. Players need a clear way to Tap and Untap their lands, and actions must be reflected in the log and visually. Lands may remain tapped across multiple turns, which is incorrect.

Card Play Errors: Players are often unable to play cards even when the necessary mana appears to be available. Conversely, a creature was once played incorrectly in the Draw Phase.

The game is currently "very boring" because nothing can be played or done.

III. Game Log and Rules Enforcement
The game log is failing to communicate crucial information.

Rule Explanations in the Log (Mandatory): Whenever an action is disallowed, the log must explicitly state the reason why. This includes when a player cannot play a card, attempts an action in the wrong phase, or lacks the required mana.

Combat Phase Failure: The Combat Phase does not work at all. Creatures cannot attack, and tapping them during the phase has no effect. This ruins the game.

Player Cheating (Log Error): Clicking on an opponent's face-down card reveals its name and type in the game log. This is a severe bug that gives players an unfair advantage.

Card Options: Cards on the battlefield need clear, visible options for interaction, such as "Tap for Mana," "Declare as Attacker," or "Activate [Special Ability]".

IV. Miscellaneous and Technical Issues
Deck Management: Pre-generated decks should have an indicator if they are not full/valid. Players should be allowed to click and arrange the cards in their hand in their preferred order.
